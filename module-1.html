01 — প্রযুক্তির দায়িত্বশীল ব্যবহার

Responsible Use of Technology | 20 minutes

📊
Did you know? Bangladesh has over 40 million active social media users. Young people aged 15–24 spend an average of 3–5 hours per day on their phones — most of it unplanned.

Key Ideas

  • Technology is a tool, not a lifestyle. A phone is like a hammer — useful, but you wouldn't hold it 6 hours a day just because you own one.
  • Attention is a resource. Every app you open is competing for your focus.
  • Screen time ≠ value time. Video-calling a parent is different from doom-scrolling for an hour.
  • Responsible use starts with awareness, not guilt.
  • What we do repeatedly becomes who we are. Habits are built by repetition.
📖 Classroom Story — Rafi Rafi is a Class 11 student in Mymensingh. He gets home at 4pm. Plans to study at 5pm. Opens Facebook — just for 10 minutes. Looks up. It's 7pm. Books still closed. Rafi wasn't lazy. He just wasn't making any decision — and the app made it for him.

Think About This

When did you last use your phone without meaning to? What were you supposed to be doing?
What would you do with an extra hour if your phone didn't exist?
Is there a type of phone use that actually makes you feel better after? What is it?
