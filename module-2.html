02 — ডিজিটাল দায়িত্ববোধ

Digital Responsibility | 25 minutes

🌐
Did you know? A WhatsApp message can reach thousands of people within minutes. In Bangladesh, false health warnings, election rumors, and fake news have spread this way — and real people have been affected. Once you share something, you cannot fully unsend it.

Key Ideas

  • Your digital footprint is permanent. Everything you post, share, or like leaves a trace — even if you delete it.
  • Online behavior is real behavior. Saying something hurtful online is not less real because it's on a screen.
  • Sharing = responsibility. When you forward information, you become part of its chain. If it's false, you helped spread it.
  • Cyberbullying counts. Teasing in a group chat, sharing a photo without permission — these all count.
  • Privacy matters — yours and others'. Screenshotting private conversations is a violation of digital trust.
📖 Classroom Story — Nila Nila sees a shocking celebrity story on Facebook. She re-shares it: 'এটা সত্যিই হয়েছে? / Did this really happen?' Within hours, 80 people share her post. Three days later — the story was completely fabricated. But her share is still out there. The '?' didn't protect her from being part of the chain.

Think About This

Have you ever shared something online and later realized it might not be true? What did you do?
What would you do if a friend shared your photo without asking?
Is there a difference between joking online and joking in person? Why or why not?
What does your social media profile say about you to a stranger?
